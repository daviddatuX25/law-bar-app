# Philippine Bar Examination Source Documents & Reference Materials Research
**Application Target:** Bar Exam Retrieval Funnel (Ingestion & Schema Mapping)

This report details the core statutory codes, standard textbooks, official public source repositories, and past examination resources for each of the eight (8) Philippine Bar Examination subjects. It also provides ingestion recommendations mapped to the database schema defined in `schema.sql`.

---

## 1. Civil Law (CIV)

### Core Statutory Codes (Codals)
*   **Civil Code of the Philippines (Republic Act No. 386)**
    *   *Total Articles:* 2,270 articles.
    *   *Note:* Major portions of Book I (Persons) have been repealed and replaced by the Family Code.
*   **The Family Code of the Philippines (Executive Order No. 209)**
    *   *Total Articles:* 256 articles.
*   **Property Registration Decree (Presidential Decree No. 1529)**
    *   *Total Sections:* 120 sections.
*   **The Condominium Act (Republic Act No. 4726)**
    *   *Total Sections:* 30 sections.
*   **Subdivision and Condominium Buyers' Protective Decree (Presidential Decree No. 957)**
    *   *Total Sections:* 42 sections.
*   **Domestic Administrative Adoption and Child Care Act (Republic Act No. 11642)**
    *   *Total Sections:* 57 sections (supersedes prior administrative/judicial adoption provisions of RA 8552).

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers
*   **Arturo M. Tolentino** – *Commentaries and Jurisprudence on the Civil Code of the Philippines* (Vols. I–V). The authoritative academic gold standard for theoretical foundation.
*   **Eduardo L. Paras** – *Civil Code of the Philippines Annotated* (Vols. I–V). Conversational, case-rich, and highly accessible for law school semesters.
*   **Elmer T. Rabuya** – *Civil Law Reviewer* (Vols. I & II) and *Persons and Family Relations Law*. Highly popular contemporary review books integrating recent jurisprudence.
*   **Desiderio P. Jurado** – *Civil Law Reviewer*. Classic synthesis, widely utilized for bar prep.
*   **UST Golden Notes / San Beda Memory Aid** – Critical student-compiled outlines mapping the bar syllabus.

### Public & Official Source Sourcing
*   **RA 386 (Civil Code):** [Lawphil (HTML)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra1949/ra_386_1949.html) | [ChanRobles](https://www.chanrobles.com/civilcodeofthephilippines.htm)
*   **EO 209 (Family Code):** [Official Gazette (PDF/HTML)](https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/1987/07/06/executive-order-no-209-s-1987/)
*   **PD 1529 (Property Registration):** [Lawphil (HTML)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/presdecs/pd1978/pd_1529_1978.html)

## 2. Criminal Law (CRIM)

### Core Statutory Codes (Codals)
*   **Revised Penal Code (Act No. 3815, as amended)**
    *   *Total Articles:* 367 articles (Article 365 is the last substantive felony provision; Articles 366–367 cover transitional/repealing clauses).
    *   *Book I:* General Provisions, Criminal Liability, Penalties (Articles 1–113).
    *   *Book II:* Specific Felonies and Penalties (Articles 114–367).
*   **Core Special Penal Laws (SPLs):**
    *   *Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002 (RA 9165):* 102 sections.
    *   *Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act (RA 3019):* 16 sections.
    *   *Anti-Violence Against Women and Their Children Act of 2004 (RA 9262):* 49 sections.
    *   *Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 (RA 10175):* 31 sections.
    *   *Safe Spaces Act / Bawal Bastos Law (RA 11313):* 40 sections.
    *   *Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2001 (RA 9160):* 24 sections.
    *   *Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020 (RA 11479):* 57 sections.

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers
*   **Luis B. Reyes** – *The Revised Penal Code Criminal Law* (Books 1 & 2). The absolute baseline textbook used by virtually all Philippine law schools.
*   **Justice Florenz D. Regalado** – *Criminal Law Conspectus*. Highly authoritative, particularly regarding complex penalty computations.
*   **Atty. Campanilla / Ortega / Boado** – Popular reviewers designed specifically for SPL breakdowns and rapid bar recall.

### Public & Official Source Sourcing
*   **Act 3815 (Revised Penal Code):** [Lawphil (HTML)](https://lawphil.net/statutes/acts/act_3815_1930.html) | [Official Gazette](https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/)
*   **RA 9165 (Dangerous Drugs Act):** [Official Gazette (HTML)](https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2002/06/07/republic-act-no-9165/)
*   **RA 3019 (Anti-Graft Act):** [Official Gazette](https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/1960/08/17/republic-act-no-3019/)

    *   *Total Sections:* 264 sections.
*   **Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees (RA 6713)**

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers

*   **Antonio E.B. Nachura** – *Outline Reviewer in Political Law*. The standard bar review companion summarizing all political sub-branches.
*   **Atty. Rene B. Gorospe** – *Political Law*. Highly structured for modern case application.

## 4. Remedial Law (REM)

### Core Statutory Codes (Codals)
*   **Rules of Court of the Philippines (Rules 1 to 144)**
    *   *Civil Procedure (Rules 1–71):* Governed by the **2019 Amendments to the 1997 Rules of Civil Procedure** (A.M. No. 19-10-20-SC).
    *   *Criminal Procedure (Rules 110–127):* Governed by the **2000 Revised Rules of Criminal Procedure** (A.M. No. 00-5-03-SC).
    *   *Evidence (Rules 128–134):* Governed by the **2019 Revised Rules on Evidence** (A.M. No. 19-08-15-SC).
    *   *Special Proceedings (Rules 72–109):* Covers wills, estates, guardianships, and adoptions.
*   **Special Writs and Rules:**
    *   *Writ of Amparo:* A.M. No. 07-9-12-SC.
    *   *Writ of Habeas Data:* A.M. No. 08-1-16-SC.
    *   *Rules of Procedure for Environmental Cases (Writ of Kalikasan):* A.M. No. 09-6-8-SC.

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers
*   **Dean Willard B. Riano** – *Civil Procedure*, *Criminal Procedure*, and *Evidence* (Bar Lecture Series). Unmatched clarity for establishing a procedural foundation.
*   **Justice Florenz D. Regalado** – *Remedial Law Compendium* (Vols. I & II). Deeply authoritative reference.
*   **Dean Salvador N. Moya II** – *Syllabus-Based Reviewers in Remedial Law*. Formatted precisely to match the SC syllabus.
*   **Dean Ferdinand A. Tan** – *Civil Procedure: A Guide for the Bench and the Bar*. Heavily oriented toward flowcharting and procedural timelines.

### Public & Official Source Sourcing
*   **Rules of Court & Amendments:** [Supreme Court E-Library](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/) (Search by Administrative Matter number for exact, official amendatory texts).
*   **Rules of Court (Historical Baseline):** [Lawphil](https://lawphil.net/courts/rules/rc_1-71_civref.html)

## 6. Labor Law (LAB)

### Core Statutory Codes (Codals)
*   **Labor Code of the Philippines (Presidential Decree No. 442, as renumbered)**
    *   *Total Articles:* 302 active articles (originally 334 prior to Department Order No. 40-03 and structural consolidations).
    *   *Structure:* Books I (Pre-Employment), II (HRD), III (Conditions of Employment), IV (Health & Social Welfare), V (Labor Relations), VI (Post-Employment), VII (Transitory Provisions).
*   **Social Legislation:**
    *   *Social Security Act of 2018 (RA 11199):* 32 sections.
    *   *Government Service Insurance System Act of 1997 (RA 8291):* 58 sections.
    *   *Universal Health Care Act (RA 11223):* 48 sections.

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers
*   **Cesario A. Azucena Jr.** – *The Labor Code with Comments and Cases* (Vols. I & II). The absolute semester authority.
*   **Atty. Joselito Guianan Chan** – *Labor Law Reviewer* and *Bar Reviewer on Labor Law*. Heavily updated and detailed source for both codals and DOLE Department Orders.
*   **Atty. Duano** – *Labor Law Digest / Q&A Reviewer*. Focused on bar-style problem solving.

### Public & Official Source Sourcing
*   **PD 442 (Labor Code):** [Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) Portal](https://www.dole.gov.ph/labor-code-of-the-philippines/) (Maintains the official renumbered edition).

## 7. Taxation (TAX)

### Core Statutory Codes (Codals)
*   **National Internal Revenue Code of 1997 (Republic Act No. 8424)**
    *   *Structure:* Titles I to XIV (covering Income Tax, VAT, Excise Taxes, Donor's/Estate Taxes, Administrative Penalties).
    *   *Amendments:* Heavily modified by the **TRAIN Law** (RA 10963), **CREATE Act** (RA 11534), and **Ease of Paying Taxes (EOPT) Act** (RA 11976).
*   **Local Government Code of 1991 (RA 7160) - Book II**
    *   *Scope:* Local Government Taxation and Real Property Taxation (Sections 128 to 283).
*   **Customs Modernization and Tariff Act (CMTA) (Republic Act No. 10863)**
    *   *Total Sections:* 1,804 sections.
*   **CTA Charter (RA 1125, as amended by RA 9282 and RA 9503)**
    *   *Scope:* Court of Tax Appeals jurisdiction and procedures (23 sections).

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers
*   **Justice Japar B. Dimaampao** – *Taxation Laws Explained* and *Tax Principles and Remedies*. Highly authoritative; Dimaampao is a Supreme Court Associate Justice.
*   **Atty. E.P. Ingles** – *Tax Made Less Taxing*. Extremely popular for its simplified diagrams, conversational tone, and exam-focused explanations.
*   **Victorino C. Mamalateo** – *Reviewer on Taxation*. Classic comprehensive reviewer.

### Public & Official Source Sourcing
*   **NIRC & Tax Amendments:** [Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) Legal Section](https://www.bir.gov.ph/) (The definitive source for up-to-date NIRC sections, Revenue Regulations, and circulars).
*   **CMTA (RA 10863):** [Official Gazette (PDF)](https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/2016/05/30/republic-act-no-10863/)

## 8. Legal Ethics (ETH)

### Core Statutory Codes (Codals)
*   **Code of Professional Responsibility and Accountability (CPRA) (Supreme Court A.M. No. 22-09-01-SC)**
    *   *Approved:* April 11, 2023.
    *   *Structure:* Preamble + 6 Canons (Independence, Integrity, Impartiality, Propriety, Equality, Competence and Diligence), 15 Chapters, 82 Sections.
    *   *Note:* Canon VI (Accountability) governs disciplinary actions, replacing Rule 139-B of the Rules of Court.
*   **New Code of Judicial Conduct for the Philippine Judiciary (A.M. No. 03-05-01-SC)**
    *   *Structure:* Preamble + 6 Canons.
*   **Rules of Court - Rule 138 (Attorneys and Admission to the Bar)**
    *   *Structure:* Sections 1 to 37.
*   **Rules of Court - Rule 138-A (Law Student Practice Rule)**
    *   *Structure:* Sections 1 to 5 (governing Clinical Legal Education Programs - CLEP).

### Reputable Standard Textbooks & Reviewers
*   **Ruben E. Agpalo** – *Legal and Judicial Ethics*. The definitive classic textbook in the field.
*   **Atty. Funa / Pineda** – Authors of widely recognized ethics treatises and reviewers.
*   **SC CPRA Handbook / Annotations** – Official manuals published by the Supreme Court subcommittees detailing the shift from the old CPR to the CPRA.

### Public & Official Source Sourcing
*   **CPRA (A.M. No. 22-09-01-SC):** [Supreme Court of the Philippines (PDF)](https://sc.judiciary.gov.ph/sp-files/CPRA-AM-No.-22-09-01-SC.pdf)
*   **New Code of Judicial Conduct:** [Supreme Court E-Library](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/)

## 9. Recommended Past Bar Question Datasets & Digital Resource Banks

Since the Supreme Court does not release past bar questions in clean data-science formats (such as JSON or CSV), developers of retrieval funnels must scrape or parse documents from:

1.  **The Lawphil Project Bar Archive:**
    *   *URL:* [lawphil.net/judjuris/juri_bar.html](https://lawphil.net/judjuris/juri_bar.html)
    *   *Coverage:* Full text of questionnaires from 1997 to recent years.
2.  **UST Law "In Focus" Bar Q&A:**
    *   *URL:* [ustcivillaw.com](http://www.ustcivillaw.com/)
    *   *Contents:* Compilation of past bar questions and suggested answers sorted by year and subject.
3.  **UP Law Center (Suggested Answers):**
    *   *Nature:* The official "gold standard" reference published in print volumes. Useful for OCR/ingestion to populate the `common_confusion` and `decoy_pairs` tables.
4.  **Supreme Court Bar Bulletins and Recent Questionnaires:**
    *   *URL:* [sc.judiciary.gov.ph](https://sc.judiciary.gov.ph/) under the Bar Examination tab.

## 10. Database Schema Mapping & Ingestion Recommendations

To integrate these documents into the schema defined in `schema.sql`, use the following guidelines:

### A. Subject Mapping (`subjects` Table)
```json
[
  {"id": "CIV", "name": "Civil Law"},
  {"id": "CRIM", "name": "Criminal Law"},
  {"id": "POL", "name": "Political Law"},
  {"id": "REM", "name": "Remedial Law"},
  {"id": "COMM", "name": "Commercial Law"},
  {"id": "LAB", "name": "Labor Law"},
  {"id": "TAX", "name": "Taxation"},
  {"id": "ETH", "name": "Legal Ethics"}
]
```

### B. Segmenting Codals for `source_paragraphs`
*   **Granularity:** Store each **Article** (e.g., Civil Code Art. 2176) or **Section** (e.g., Corporation Code Sec. 22) as a single row in the `source_paragraphs` table.
*   **Anchor Identification:** Format `anchor_id` systematically (e.g., `cc-art-2176`, `fc-art-26`, `rcp-rule-110-sec-1`). This allows the retrieval engine to pull exact matches.

### C. Constructing `provisions` Elements Checklists (JSON String Arrays)
When ingesting codal provisions into the `provisions` table, parse the text to extract core elements:
*   *Example:* **Article 2176, Civil Code (Quasi-Delict)**
    *   `citation`: "Civil Code, Art. 2176"
    *   `short_title`: "Quasi-Delict / Tort"
    *   `elements_checklist`: `["Act or omission", "Damage or injury", "Fault or negligence", "Causal connection between fault/negligence and damage", "No pre-existing contractual relation between the parties"]`
    *   `common_confusion`: "Confusing Quasi-Delict with Culpa Contractual (breach of contract) or Culpa Criminal (criminal negligence)."
    *   `distinguishing_fact`: "Check if there is an existing contract between the parties. If yes, the action is generally for breach of contract, not quasi-delict."
