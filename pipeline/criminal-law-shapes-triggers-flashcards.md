# Criminal Law — Shapes, Trigger Words, Flashcards
Generated from Revised Penal Code (Act 3815) and RA 9165

---

CARD crim-1
FRONT (shape): A person commits a felony through a willful and intentional act with deliberate intent to cause harm.
FRONT (trigger words): intent, malice, dolus, deliberate intent, willful, intentional felony
BACK (provision): Art. 3 & 4 - Felonies Defined; Criminal Intent (Dolo)
BACK (elements):
1. There is an act or omission
2. The act is punishable by law (nullum crimen sine lege)
3. The act is performed with deliberate intent (dolo) — malice or willfulness
4. The act produces a felonious result
5. Criminal liability attaches even if the result is different from the intent (Art. 4, par. 1: praeter intentionem)
BACK (common confusion): Art. 365 (Imprudence and Negligence) :: Dolo requires intent and malice; culpa (Art. 365) requires negligence or lack of foresight without intent. The two are mutually exclusive for the same act.
SOURCE: RPC Arts. 3, 4; Bar 2018 Q6

CARD crim-2
FRONT (shape): A person commits a felony through reckless imprudence, negligence, or lack of foresight without any intent to cause harm.
FRONT (trigger words): culpa, negligence, imprudence, reckless, lack of foresight, quasi-offense
BACK (provision): Art. 365 - Imprudence and Negligence (Culpa)
BACK (elements):
1. There is an act or omission
2. The act is performed without intent to cause harm
3. The act is done with reckless imprudence (lack of precaution) or simple negligence (lack of foresight)
4. The act causes damage or injury to another
5. The negligence is the proximate cause of the injury
BACK (common confusion): Art. 3-4 (Dolo) :: Culpa is the absence of intent — the person did not mean to commit the wrong. Dolo requires deliberate intent. A person who negligently runs over someone is liable under Art. 365, not for intentional homicide.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 365

CARD crim-3
FRONT (shape): Two or more persons conspire to commit a felony, and the conspiracy itself is punished even if the felony is not actually perpetrated.
FRONT (trigger words): conspiracy, plot, agreement to commit felony, punishable conspiracy, criminal confederation
BACK (provision): Art. 8 - Conspiracy and Proposal to Commit Felony
BACK (elements):
1. Two or more persons come to an agreement
2. The agreement is to commit a felony
3. There is a decision or resolution to commit it
4. For conspiracy to be punishable: the law expressly penalizes the conspiracy itself (e.g. treason, rebellion)
5. If not expressly penalized, conspiracy is only a basis for vicarious liability when the felony is actually committed
BACK (common confusion): Art. 17-18 (Principals by Inducement) :: In conspiracy, all conspirators are liable as principals regardless of who actually performed the act. Proposal (Art. 8, par. 2) is one person proposing to another to commit a felony — it is generally not punishable unless the law so provides.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 8

CARD crim-4
FRONT (shape): A person kills another without any justifying circumstance, attended by qualifying circumstances like treachery or evident premeditation.
FRONT (trigger words): murder, killing, treachery, evident premeditation, alevosia, abuse of superior strength
BACK (provision): Art. 248 - Murder
BACK (elements):
1. A person is killed
2. The killing is not parricide or infanticide
3. The killing is not justified or exempted
4. At least one qualifying circumstance attends the killing: (a) treachery, (b) evident premeditation, (c) cruelty, (d) price/reward, (e) fire/flood/poison, (f) abuse of superior strength, (g) with aid of armed men
5. The offender had intent to kill
BACK (common confusion): Art. 249 (Homicide) :: Homicide is the killing of a person WITHOUT any qualifying circumstance. If ANY qualifying circumstance under Art. 248 is present, it is murder. The presence or absence of the four circumstances determines which crime.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 248; Bar 2021 Q9

CARD crim-5
FRONT (shape): A person kills another in the heat of passion or upon a provocation sufficiently grave, without any qualifying circumstances.
FRONT (trigger words): homicide, killing, heat of passion, no qualifying circumstance, death
BACK (provision): Art. 249 - Homicide
BACK (elements):
1. A person is killed
2. The killing is not parricide, murder, or infanticide
3. No qualifying circumstance under Art. 248 is present
4. No justifying or exempting circumstance exists
5. The offender had intent to kill
BACK (common confusion): Art. 248 (Murder) :: The sole difference between homicide and murder is the presence of a qualifying circumstance. If none of the seven circumstances in Art. 248 exist, it is homicide under Art. 249. If at least one exists, it is murder.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 249

CARD crim-6
FRONT (shape): A person kills his or her legitimate parent, child, or spouse.
FRONT (trigger words): parricide, parent, child, spouse, ascendant, descendant, legitimate relation
BACK (provision): Art. 246 - Parricide
BACK (elements):
1. A person is killed
2. The killer is the legitimate father, mother, child (legitimate or illegitimate), or spouse of the deceased
3. The relationship existed at the time of the killing
4. The offender had intent to kill
5. The killing is not justified or exempted
BACK (common confusion): Art. 248 (Murder) :: If the victim is a legitimate parent and the killer is the child, it is PARRICIDE, not murder — even if treachery is present. The relationship absorbs the qualifying circumstance. Murder applies when no such relationship exists.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 246; Bar 2019 Q5

CARD crim-7
FRONT (shape): A person takes personal property belonging to another without consent, with intent to gain, and without violence or intimidation.
FRONT (trigger words): theft, personal property, without consent, intent to gain, no violence, taking
BACK (provision): Art. 308-309 - Theft
BACK (elements):
1. There is taking of personal property
2. The property belongs to another
3. The taking is without the owner's consent
4. The taking is done with intent to gain (animus lucrandi)
5. The taking is accomplished without violence, intimidation, or force upon things
6. The property is taken without the use of a grave threat (otherwise it becomes robbery)
BACK (common confusion): Art. 293 (Robbery) :: Theft is taking WITHOUT violence or intimidation. If violence, intimidation, or force upon things is used to take the property, it is robbery — a more serious crime with higher penalties.
SOURCE: RPC Arts. 308-309

CARD crim-8
FRONT (shape): A person takes personal property belonging to another with intent to gain, using violence or intimidation against the person or force upon things.
FRONT (trigger words): robbery, violence, intimidation, force upon things, intent to gain, taking with violence
BACK (provision): Art. 293-294 - Robbery (With Violence or Intimidation)
BACK (elements):
1. There is taking of personal property
2. The property belongs to another
3. The taking is with intent to gain
4. The taking is accomplished through violence or intimidation against the person (Art. 293) OR force upon things
5. The violence or intimidation precedes or accompanies the taking
BACK (common confusion): Art. 308-309 (Theft) :: Robbery = theft + violence/intimidation. If the property is taken without any violence or intimidation, the crime is theft. If the violence is used AFTER the taking to escape, it may be robbery or a separate crime depending on the facts.
SOURCE: RPC Arts. 293-294

CARD crim-9
FRONT (shape): A person by means of false pretenses or fraudulent acts induces another to give money or property, causing damage.
FRONT (trigger words): estafa, swindling, false pretenses, fraudulent, deceit, damage, misrepresentation
BACK (provision): Art. 315 - Estafa (Swindling)
BACK (elements):
1. The accused defrauded another by using false pretenses or fraudulent acts
2. The false pretense or fraudulent act was made prior to or simultaneously with the crime
3. The offended party relied on the false pretense and parted with money or property
4. Damage or prejudice is caused to the offended party
5. The amount of fraud determines the penalty
BACK (common confusion): Art. 316 (Other Forms of Swindling) :: Estafa under Art. 315 requires that the false pretense be the CAUSE that induced the victim to part with property. Under Art. 316, the fraud involves abuse of confidence in property administration.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 315

CARD crim-10
FRONT (shape): A public officer, with grave abuse of discretion, acts arbitrarily and causes injustice or injury to a private person.
FRONT (trigger words): public officer, grave abuse, oppression, injustice, arbitrary detention, abuse of authority
BACK (provision): Art. 204-211 - Crimes Committed by Public Officers (Usurpation of Authority)
BACK (elements):
1. The offender is a public officer
2. The officer commits an arbitrary act or detains a person without legal grounds
3. The act is committed with grave abuse of discretion
4. The act results in injustice, injury, or violation of rights
5. The officer is not authorized by law to perform the act
BACK (common confusion): RA 3019 (Anti-Graft Law) :: RPC Art. 204-211 punishes specific acts of abuse by public officers. RA 3019 punishes corrupt practices of public officers with broader elements including giving unwarranted benefit to private parties.
SOURCE: RPC Arts. 204-211

CARD crim-11
FRONT (shape): A person engages in sexual intercourse with another without consent through force, threat, or intimidation, or when the victim is deprived of reason.
FRONT (trigger words): rape, sexual intercourse, force, intimidation, threat, without consent, deprived of reason
BACK (provision): Art. 266-A (as amended by RA 8353) - Rape
BACK (elements):
1. There is carnal knowledge (sexual intercourse)
2. The act is accomplished through: (a) force, threat, or intimidation, OR (b) when the victim is deprived of reason or unconscious, OR (c) when the victim is under 12 years of age
3. The offender had intent to have sexual intercourse
4. Consent, if any, is vitiated by the force, threat, or circumstance
5. Sexual assault (par. 2): insertion of any instrument or object into the genital or anal orifice
BACK (common confusion): Acts of Lasciviousness (Art. 336) :: Rape requires sexual intercourse (penile penetration of the genital organ). Acts of lasciviousness covers other lewd acts without penetration. If there is no carnal knowledge, it is not rape.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 266-A, as amended; Bar 2020 Q3

CARD crim-12
FRONT (shape): A person is caught in possession of dangerous drugs or drug paraphernalia without the required authorization.
FRONT (trigger words): illegal drugs, possession, dangerous drugs, shabu, marijuana, unauthorized, drug paraphernalia
BACK (provision): RA 9165 Sec. 11 & 12 - Illegal Possession of Dangerous Drugs and Drug Paraphernalia
BACK (elements):
1. The accused is found in possession of a dangerous drug or drug paraphernalia
2. The possession is without legal authority or prescription
3. The accused knowingly possessed the item
4. The quantity of the drug determines the penalty (Sec. 11)
5. For paraphernalia (Sec. 12): any equipment used for ingesting, smoking, injecting drugs
BACK (common confusion): RA 9165 Sec. 5 (Sale of Drugs) :: Possession is a separate crime from sale. For sale (Sec. 5), the prosecution must prove the elements of a sale transaction. Possession (Secs. 11-12) only requires proof of physical or constructive possession without authority.
SOURCE: RA 9165 Secs. 11-12; Bar 2022 Q10

CARD crim-13
FRONT (shape): A person kills another in a quarrel or fight, where the deceased was the initial aggressor but the accused used disproportionate force in retaliation.
FRONT (trigger words): physical injuries, serious physical injuries, wound, fracture, incapacitation, deformation
BACK (provision): Art. 263-266 - Serious Physical Injuries
BACK (elements):
1. There is a physical injury inflicted upon another person
2. The injury is serious in nature — causes deformity, incapacity, illness exceeding 30 days
3. The injury is caused without intent to kill
4. If the wound would have been fatal but medical intervention saved the victim, it is still frustrated homicide, not physical injuries
BACK (common confusion): Frustrated Homicide vs. Physical Injuries :: If intent to kill is present and the wound would be fatal without medical help, it is frustrated homicide (Art. 6 + 249). If there is no intent to kill, it is physical injuries. The wound's location and weapon used are key evidentiary factors.
SOURCE: RPC Arts. 263-266

CARD crim-14
FRONT (shape): A person who was compelled by an irresistible force or who acted under the impulse of an uncontrollable fear of equal or greater injury avoids criminal liability.
FRONT (trigger words): exempting circumstance, irresistible force, uncontrollable fear, duress, no criminal liability
BACK (provision): Art. 12 par. 4-5 - Exempting Circumstances (Duress and Irresistible Force)
BACK (elements):
1. Irresistible force (par. 4): The accused acts under a force that physically compels the act; there is no voluntariness whatsoever
2. Uncontrollable fear (par. 5): The accused acts under a threat of an equal or greater injury that produces an uncontrollable fear
3. The fear must be real and imminent
4. The injury threatened must be equal or greater than the injury caused
5. The accused could not resist or avoid the threat
BACK (common confusion): Art. 11 par. 4 (Justifying Circumstance — Self-Defense) :: Exempting circumstances remove criminal liability entirely because there is no voluntariness or intent. Self-defense (Art. 11) justifies the act because the person was defending themselves — it requires reasonable necessity of the means employed.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 12, pars. 4-5

CARD crim-15
FRONT (shape): A person defends himself, his rights, or another person from an unlawful aggression, using reasonable means to repel it.
FRONT (trigger words): self-defense, defense of relative, defense of stranger, unlawful aggression, reasonable means
BACK (provision): Art. 11 - Justifying Circumstances (Self-Defense)
BACK (elements):
1. Unlawful aggression — an actual or imminent threat
2. Reasonable necessity of the means employed to prevent or repel the aggression
3. Lack of sufficient provocation on the part of the person defending himself
4. For defense of relatives: the same elements + relationship exists
5. For defense of strangers: the same elements + the defender is not induced by revenge or evil motive
BACK (common confusion): Art. 12 (Exempting Circumstances) :: Justifying circumstances make the act LAWFUL — there is no crime. Exempting circumstances make the person NOT LIABLE even if the act is a crime (no voluntariness). In self-defense, the burden of proof shifts to the accused to prove all elements by clear and convincing evidence.
SOURCE: RPC Art. 11; Bar 2017 Q12

SHARED_TRIGGER: intentional vs negligent harm
DISTINQUISHING_FACT: Dolo (crim-1) requires **deliberate intent and malice** — the person meant to do the harm. Culpa (crim-2) requires **negligence or imprudence without intent** — the person did not intend the harm but failed to exercise due care. They are mutually exclusive: the same act cannot be both dolo and culpa.

If ANY qualifying circumstance is present → Murder (Art. 248). If NONE → Homicide (Art. 249).
